# **CALLVAULT**

**CallVault is the universal call vault for your team – it pulls every recording from every tool into organization‑ and workspace‑based hubs, then lets any AI access it via MCP.**  
The product doesn’t *do* the thinking. It gives your AIs a clean, permissioned memory.

You’re not an “AI notes app.” You’re a **conversation OS / data layer**. That’s a different category. That’s how you sell in a vacuum instead of being compared to every other AI notetaker. 

**1\. Category & Core Promise**

**Category:**  
“AI‑ready conversation OS” or “Universal call vault for teams.”

**One‑sentence positioning:**

“CallVault centralizes every call from every tool into secure workspaces, then gives Claude, ChatGPT, and your custom agents MCP access so your AI actually knows what happened on your calls.”

**Core promise:**

“One place for all your team’s calls, organized the way your business works, with clean, permissioned access for both humans and AI.”

## **2\. The Value Prop, Broken Down**

### **Pillar 1: Universal aggregation (source‑agnostic)**

* Connect Fathom, Zoom, Meet, YouTube, uploads, Loom, etc.  
* Every user can keep using their favorite recorder; everything lands in one **Organization → Workspace → Folder → Call** hierarchy.  
* This solves the “5 tools, 5 silos, no shared context” problem.

### **Pillar 2: Workspace‑based organization & sharing**

Use the boring, obvious mental model users already understand (this is where metaphors beat cute jargon). \[$100M Playbook Proof Checklist, Page 24\]

* **Organizations**: Hard walls. “Personal,” “Acme Corp,” “UGC Mastery Coaching.” Separate data, billing, permissions.  
* **Workspaces**: Shared hubs *inside* an org. Can represent a **team, client, or program**:  
  * “Sales – US,” “Cohort – March 2026,” “Client – Acme Corp.”  
* **Folders**: Just structure inside a workspace. No new permission layer.  
* **Calls**: The atomic unit.

What this unlocks:

* Coaches: one org for the coaching business; a workspace per cohort/program/client. Students see only their workspace; coach still sees everything.  
* Companies: one org per company, workspaces per team / region / client segment.  
* Solos: “Personal” org with as many workspaces as they want.

The user mental model:

“I choose the **organization** I’m in, then the **workspace** (team / program / client), then I see all the calls I should see.”

### **Pillar 3: MCP as the intelligence interface**

* Each **workspace** can expose tools/resources over MCP:  
  * search calls, get transcript, get recording\_context, browse folders, speaker history, etc.  
* Users plug that MCP URL into Claude / ChatGPT / Cursor / agents.  
* Now they can ask:  
  * “Summarize everything we’ve discussed with Acme Corp.”  
  * “Show me every pricing objection in this workspace last month.”  
* Your positioning line here:

   “The AI doesn’t live in CallVault. CallVault is the memory layer your AI plugs into.”

That’s your “unique mechanism” in consumer language, not jargon. 

**3\. How pricing maps to the story**

* **Free:** Personal sandbox.

  * 1 org, 1 workspace, 10 imports / month, basic smart import.  
  * No MCP. Just enough to feel aggregation and organization.  
* **Pro ($29):** Serious individual / coach.

  * 1 user, unlimited imports, multiple workspaces, full MCP, 1,000 AI actions / month.  
  * “Your personal and client workspaces, plus your AI plugged into all of them.”  
* **Team ($79):** Small team / mid‑market.

  * 3–10 users, shared org, roles/permissions, 5,000 pooled AI actions.  
  * “One organization, many workspaces, your whole team \+ their AIs on top.”

You charge for **organized, permissioned, always‑on memory**, not for trying to out‑AI Anthropic or OpenAI. That’s the whole play.
